Hey There. Welcome to our Analysis of AirBnB data.

We are four graduate students from University of Washington Information School. As a part of the Open Datathon by Citadel, we analysed the listing trends and built strategies for new onboarding airbnb hosts to improve their experience of becoming a successful and profitable host. In this website, you can find the problem, then the comprehensive analysis and finally the business recommendations, in that order.

  • The Problem
  • The Analysis
  • The Conclusion

Airbnb listings are consolidated in and around metropolitan cities, where real estate prices are higher than median prices. Airbnb in the last 10 years has captured data about the following:

1) Tourist Movement
2) Tourist Sentiment and
3) House Reviews.
These resources can provide a good head start to begin a culture where people explore unexplored towns and cities. AirBnB can use its massive data to empower hosts in these unexplored areas to join the network through a data driven approach of building amazing experiences at affordable prices. In a nut shell, now that AirBnB knows what has worked well for customers in the last decade , how can it leverage that knowledge to help the next generation of AirBnB Hosts to provide experiences that work.

The Problem

How can AirBNB leverage its data to empower new and existing hosts, to shift focus from metropolitan listings and expand its network of homes.



Proposed Solution

We want to build “analytics as as service” product called “Host Services” for existing and new hosts that helps them onboard and succeed at hosting on AirBnB quickly by letting them focus on their offered features that work well with the customers.


  • 1. How can we help new onboarding hosts to decide which amenities and feedback can result in better user reviews.
  • 2. How can quantitative features of a hosts listing and demographic can result in better review ratings.
  • 3. Are there any interesting patterns over time of AirBnB listing from 2015 to 2018 which can help us optimize the listings.

  • 3. We explored the calendar table to find out what kind of data is available to us for a time series analysis. We found out that although the dates and data is alright, there are a large number of na values for price.
